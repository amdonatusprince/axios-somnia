`PayrollTreasury` is the on-chain vault where employers hold **MUSD** before payroll. It uses explicit ERC-20 accounting (no native-balance shortcuts): **`getAvailableBalance`**, **`getLockedBalance`**, and per-employer state in the `employers` mapping.

## Employer identity

Each employer account is keyed by:

```text
employerId = keccak256(abi.encodePacked(msg.sender))
```

The **first deposit** from an address sets that address as **`admin`** for that `employerId`. Subsequent deposits from the same address add to **`balance`**.

## Operations

### Deposit

`deposit(uint256 amount, bytes32 memo)` — `transferFrom` MUSD from `msg.sender` into the contract and credit **`balance`**.

### Withdraw

`withdraw(uint256 amount)` — moves **available** MUSD back to **`msg.sender`**. Requires:

- `amount > 0`
- Caller is the registered **`admin`** for `keccak256(abi.encodePacked(msg.sender))`
- **`balance` ≥ amount** (only **available** funds; **locked** payroll collateral is not withdrawable until unlocked by the batcher)

### Gas budget

`fundGasBudget(uint256 amount)` — separate accounting for operational gas (ERC-20 held by the contract).

### Batcher-only

`lockFunds`, `releaseTo`, `unlockFunds` — called by **PayrollBatcher** to move value between **available** and **locked**, and to pay employees.

## Token decimals

MUSD on Mezo uses **18 decimals**. Format amounts in the app with the same precision as on-chain `uint256` values.

## Dashboard

The employer app supports **deposit** (approve + deposit) and **withdraw** (single transaction) when the deployed contract includes `withdraw`. After a new deployment, update **`NEXT_PUBLIC_PAYROLL_TREASURY`** and any **`employers.treasury_contract`** rows in Supabase.
